What is your return policy?

Return Window: Many retailers allow returns within a specific timeframe, often ranging from 14 to 90 days from the date of purchase. Condition of Items: Items often need to be returned in their original condition, which typically means unused, unworn, and in original packaging. Some sites may also accept returns of opened items if they are defective. Refund Methods: Refunds are usually issued to the original payment method. Some retailers may offer store credit or exchanges instead. Return Shipping: Policies may specify whether the customer or the retailer pays for return shipping. Some retailers offer free returns, while others may deduct return shipping costs from the refund. Exclusions: Certain items, such as final sale items, personalized products, or perishable goods, may be non-returnable. Return Process: Most retailers provide instructions for how to initiate a return, which may include printing a return label, filling out a return form, or contacting customer service.
